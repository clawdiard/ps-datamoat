The Moat Framework

Why B2B data compounds while media degrades ‚Äî scored on five dimensions of competitive durability.

Switching Costs

9.2
Data companies embed in workflows. Ripping out Verisk or RELX costs millions and years. Media subscriptions cancel with a click.

Network Effects

7.8
More data ‚Üí better models ‚Üí more customers ‚Üí more data. CoStar and S&P Global exhibit strong data network effects.

Regulatory Lock-In

8.5
Credit ratings, insurance data, and compliance tools are mandated by regulation. You can't unbundle what the law requires.

Pricing Power

8.9
Data companies raise prices 5-8% annually with minimal churn. Media faces constant downward pressure from free alternatives.

Data Uniqueness

9.5
Proprietary datasets built over decades are nearly impossible to replicate. Verisk's actuarial data dates to 1971. News content is commoditized in hours.

AI Resilience

6.4
AI enhances data products (better analytics) but threatens media (content summarization). Data companies are AI beneficiaries; media are AI casualties.

The regulatory layer (state insurance commissions mandate ISO data) creates a legal moat on top of the data moat. Disruption risk: Very Low.

Competitive Dynamics

Healthcare data is the most fragmented and highest-growth B2B data vertical. Three layers: (1) Clinical reference data (FDB, Zynx, MCG ‚Äî oligopolies within niches), (2) Platforms (Veeva for pharma, Health Catalyst for providers), (3) Real-world data/analytics (Flatiron, Tempus, Komodo Health). The clinical validation requirement ‚Äî FDA expectations, peer-reviewed evidence ‚Äî creates years-long barriers to entry. AI is an accelerant here, not a disruptor: you need the data to train the models. Disruption risk: Low. Growth potential: Highest of all sectors.

Competitive Dynamics

CRE data is a three-layer market: (1) Property data + analytics (CoStar dominant for listings/comps, MSCI for institutional benchmarks), (2) Operating systems (Yardi, MRI Software, RealPage ‚Äî now under Thoma Bravo), (3) Specialty verticals (Reonomy for ownership data, Cherre for data integration). CoStar's moat is deepest because of the physical research army ‚Äî you can't scrape what doesn't exist online. The residential push (Homes.com vs Zillow) is a calculated $1B+ bet that could either double CoStar's TAM or prove a costly distraction. Disruption risk: Low-Moderate. AI could automate property research but can't replace proprietary transaction data.

The Great Divergence

Key moments that separated data winners from media losers over the past two decades.

2005
Craigslist Kills Classifieds
Newspaper classified revenue drops 50% in 5 years. Data companies see no impact ‚Äî their customers are enterprises, not consumers.
2007
iPhone Launches ‚Üí Mobile Attention Shift
Media must chase eyeballs on a new platform. Data companies charge per-seat regardless of device.
2008‚Äì2009
Financial Crisis Accelerates Media Decline
Tribune, Sun-Times, Philadelphia Media file bankruptcy. Data companies barely flinch ‚Äî insurance and compliance data is non-discretionary.
2011
NYT Paywall ‚Äî A Rare Media Win
The Times proves digital subscriptions can work, but only for top-tier brands. Most papers can't replicate it.
2014
Facebook Becomes the Front Page
Publishers become dependent on platform referral traffic. Data companies sell directly ‚Äî no intermediary risk.
2016
S&P Acquires IHS Markit for $44B
The deal that defined the era ‚Äî a data merger worth more than the entire US newspaper industry combined.
2018
Local News Desert Accelerates
1,800+ newspapers closed since 2004. Meanwhile, data companies proliferate ‚Äî Verisk, CoStar, MSCI all hit all-time highs.
2020
McClatchy Files Chapter 11
A 163-year-old newspaper chain falls. That same year, RELX's market cap crosses $50B for the first time.
2021
Alden Global Acquires Tribune Publishing
Hedge funds strip-mine newspapers for cash flow. Data companies reinvest 15-20% of revenue into R&D.
2023‚Äì2025
AI Search Threatens Publisher Traffic
ChatGPT and AI summaries reduce click-throughs to news sites by 20-40%. Data companies integrate AI to enhance their products.

Strategic Insights

üìä The B2B Data Playbook

Acquire proprietary datasets ‚Üí embed in customer workflows ‚Üí raise prices annually ‚Üí compound forever. RELX, Verisk, and S&P Global have executed this for decades. Margins expand because data marginal costs approach zero.

‚ö†Ô∏è Media's Structural Disadvantage

Content is expensive to produce and instantly commoditized. A news story has a half-life of hours. A proprietary dataset has a half-life of decades. Media companies face competition from free alternatives; data companies face competition from nobody.

üîÑ The Hybrid Winners

Companies that pivoted from media to data ‚Äî News Corp (REA Group, Dow Jones data), Bloomberg (terminal + data) ‚Äî outperform pure-play media. The playbook is clear: own the data layer, not just the content layer.

ü§ñ AI: The Final Divergence

Generative AI is the ultimate threat to content businesses and the ultimate accelerant for data businesses. AI needs training data (data companies sell it) and summarizes content (media companies lose traffic). The gap will widen.

üí∞ Valuation Multiples Tell the Story

B2B data trades at 15-25x revenue. Newspapers trade at 0.5-2x revenue. The market is pricing in permanence for data and obsolescence for media. These multiples haven't converged in 15 years ‚Äî they're still diverging.

üè• Healthcare Data: The Next Frontier

Healthcare B2B data (FDB, Zynx, MCG, QGenda) mirrors the financial data playbook: regulatory mandates, workflow embedding, mission-critical usage. This sector may produce the next Verisk-scale outcomes.
